Why This Market, Why This Moment

The Los Cabos real estate market has matured beyond speculation into strategic investment. Today's acquirers are sophisticated, data-driven, and evaluating sold comps before making decisions. The era of emotional purchases in Cabo has given way to a class of buyer who applies the same rigor they'd bring to a commercial real estate allocation or alternative asset.

The Baja Peninsula's geography creates natural supply constraints. Bounded by ocean on both sides with limited developable coastal land, the corridor between San José del Cabo and Cabo San Lucas cannot expand indefinitely. Supply is structurally limited. Demand is not.

Mexico's emergence as a nearshoring hub and the normalization of remote work have expanded the buyer pool well beyond traditional vacation-home seekers. Founders, executives, and families in their prime earning years now view Los Cabos as both a lifestyle acquisition and a portfolio asset.

Transactions are conducted in U.S. dollars, reducing currency risk. Foreign ownership is straightforward through a fideicomiso (bank trust) — a government-sanctioned structure providing full property rights, backed by decades of legal precedent.
